## Budget Solo Travel in Norway

Traveling Norway on a budget is challenging but possible. This guide shows you how to maximize experiences while minimizing costs.

## Daily Budget Breakdown

Living on $110/day in Norway is challenging but possible. Here's how to make it work:

### Accommodation ($27-$49/night)
Hostels offer the best value, with dorm beds starting around $27. Budget hotels run $49+ for private rooms.

### Food ($11-$22/day)
Street food and local eateries offer meals for $11-16. Cooking in hostel kitchens saves more.

### Transportation ($11/day)
Public transit averages $11/day. Walking is free and the best way to explore.

## Money-Saving Tips

1. **Travel in shoulder season** - Lower prices and fewer crowds
2. **Cook some meals** - Hostel kitchens save significant money
3. **Free walking tours** - Tip-based tours in most cities
4. **Student/youth discounts** - Bring valid ID for discounts
5. **Happy hour specials** - Enjoy nightlife affordably

## Free Things to Do

Many cities offer free walking tours, public parks, and free museum days. Nature areas and beaches are typically free.

## Budget Accommodation Tips

Book hostels with kitchens to save on food costs. Look for weekly discounts for longer stays.

## Eating Cheap in Norway

Follow the locals - street vendors and small family restaurants offer the best value and authenticity.
