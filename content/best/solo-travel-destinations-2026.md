## The Best Places to Travel Solo in 2026

Our expert picks for solo travelers this year, based on safety ratings, value for money, ease of solo travel, and unique experiences. These destinations offer the perfect blend of adventure, culture, and security for those exploring the world on their own.

---

### 1. Japan

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Exceptionally safe, incredible food scene with solo-friendly dining (counter seating everywhere!), ultra-efficient transport, and a culture that respects privacy while remaining welcoming. The new JR Rail Pass pricing makes it more accessible than ever.

**Best for**: First-time solo travelers, food lovers, culture enthusiasts

**Budget**: $80-150/day

**2026 Highlight**: The new Hokkaido Shinkansen extension opens up more of northern Japan to easy solo exploration.

---

### 2. Portugal

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Europe's most affordable western destination, warm locals who genuinely welcome visitors, thriving digital nomad scene, and excellent safety ratings across the country.

**Best for**: Budget travelers, digital nomads, beach lovers

**Budget**: $60-120/day

**2026 Highlight**: New direct flights from multiple US cities make Lisbon and Porto more accessible than ever.

---

### 3. Slovenia

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Compact size means you can see the whole country easily, stunning Alpine and Mediterranean nature, affordable for Europe, exceptionally safe, and easy to navigate with excellent English.

**Best for**: Nature lovers, budget European travelers, outdoor enthusiasts

**Budget**: $70-120/day

**2026 Highlight**: Slovenia's new sustainable tourism initiatives make it one of Europe's greenest destinations.

---

### 4. Taiwan

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Extremely safe (consistently ranked in top 3 globally), incredibly affordable street food scene, efficient public transport, and a welcoming culture that embraces solo dining.

**Best for**: Food lovers, budget travelers, culture enthusiasts

**Budget**: $50-100/day

**2026 Highlight**: Extended visa-free access for more nationalities makes Taiwan easier to visit than ever.

---

### 5. New Zealand

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Stunning natural beauty, extremely safe, well-organized tourist infrastructure, freedom camping culture perfect for solo road trips, and some of the friendliest locals anywhere.

**Best for**: Adventure seekers, nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts

**Budget**: $100-180/day

**2026 Highlight**: New Great Walks tracks and improved hut booking systems make solo hiking more accessible.

---

### 6. Colombia

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Dramatically improved safety situation, incredible value for money, genuinely warm locals, diverse landscapes from Caribbean beaches to Andean peaks, and a thriving backpacker scene.

**Best for**: Adventure seekers, budget travelers, social travelers

**Budget**: $40-80/day

**2026 Highlight**: New digital nomad visa makes long-term stays easier for remote workers.

---

### 7. Georgia (Country)

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Remarkably safe, incredibly affordable, stunning Caucasus scenery, legendary hospitality, and a food and wine scene that rivals anywhere in the world.

**Best for**: Budget travelers, food and wine lovers, off-the-beaten-path seekers

**Budget**: $35-70/day

**2026 Highlight**: Improved mountain infrastructure opens up remote trekking areas to solo adventurers.

---

### 8. Iceland

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: One of the world's safest countries, stunning otherworldly landscapes, well-organized tours make solo exploration easy, and everyone speaks excellent English.

**Best for**: Nature lovers, adventure seekers, photographers

**Budget**: $150-250/day

**2026 Highlight**: New geothermal spa developments offer more options beyond the Blue Lagoon.

---

### 9. Mexico

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Incredible value, rich cultural heritage, amazing food scene, easy visa-free access for many nationalities, and a well-established solo traveler circuit from Mexico City to Oaxaca.

**Best for**: Food lovers, culture enthusiasts, budget travelers

**Budget**: $40-90/day

**2026 Highlight**: Expanded Tren Maya routes connect more of the Yucatan Peninsula.

---

### 10. Vietnam

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Incredibly affordable, delicious street food culture, easy north-to-south backpacker trail, rich history, and a genuine warmth from locals.

**Best for**: Budget travelers, food lovers, adventure seekers

**Budget**: $30-60/day

**2026 Highlight**: Extended e-visa validity (90 days) makes longer solo trips feasible.

---

### 11. Scotland

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Safe, English-speaking, stunning Highlands landscapes, excellent solo hiking opportunities on well-marked trails, and a pub culture where solo travelers are always welcome.

**Best for**: History buffs, nature lovers, whisky enthusiasts

**Budget**: $80-150/day

**2026 Highlight**: The North Coast 500 now has more solo-friendly accommodation options.

---

### 12. Thailand

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Budget-friendly, established backpacker trail, incredibly easy to meet other travelers, world-class beaches and islands, and some of the best street food on earth.

**Best for**: Budget travelers, first-timers, social travelers

**Budget**: $30-80/day

**2026 Highlight**: Digital Nomad Visa now available, making longer stays straightforward.

---

### 13. Spain

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Safe, excellent public transport, late-night dining culture means eating alone at 10pm is totally normal, incredible regional diversity, and welcoming locals.

**Best for**: Food lovers, culture enthusiasts, beach seekers

**Budget**: $70-140/day

**2026 Highlight**: New high-speed rail connections make island hopping by ferry even easier.

---

### 14. Morocco

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Affordable, exotic yet accessible, well-established tourist infrastructure, stunning architecture, and a unique cultural experience just hours from Europe.

**Best for**: Culture enthusiasts, budget travelers, photographers

**Budget**: $40-80/day

**2026 Highlight**: Improved train network makes getting between cities faster and easier.

---

### 15. Austria

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Exceptionally safe, stunning Alpine scenery, efficient public transport, rich cultural offerings in Vienna and Salzburg, and excellent hiking infrastructure.

**Best for**: Music lovers, winter sports enthusiasts, nature lovers

**Budget**: $90-160/day

**2026 Highlight**: New multi-day hiking routes in the Alps designed for solo trekkers.

---

### 16. South Korea

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Ultra-safe, incredible food culture, K-culture tourism at its peak, efficient transport, and a unique blend of ancient and ultra-modern.

**Best for**: K-culture fans, food lovers, tech enthusiasts

**Budget**: $60-110/day

**2026 Highlight**: Temple stay programs now offer more English-language options for solo visitors.

---

### 17. Croatia

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Safe, stunning Adriatic coastline, excellent value outside peak season, Game of Thrones tourism still going strong, and easy ferry connections between islands.

**Best for**: Beach lovers, history enthusiasts, road trippers

**Budget**: $60-120/day

**2026 Highlight**: New sailing routes make island-hopping more accessible for solo travelers.

---

### 18. Peru

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Bucket-list destinations (Machu Picchu!), incredible value, diverse landscapes from desert to jungle to mountains, and a welcoming culture.

**Best for**: Adventure seekers, history buffs, trekkers

**Budget**: $40-90/day

**2026 Highlight**: New regulations make Machu Picchu visits more sustainable and less crowded.

---

### 19. Estonia

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: One of Europe's safest countries, surprisingly affordable, stunning medieval old town in Tallinn, cutting-edge digital society, and uncrowded even in summer.

**Best for**: Digital nomads, history enthusiasts, off-the-beaten-path seekers

**Budget**: $50-100/day

**2026 Highlight**: Expanded digital nomad infrastructure makes remote work even easier.

---

### 20. Canada

**Why it's perfect for solo travelers**: Extremely safe, stunning natural beauty, multicultural cities, English-speaking, and some of the world's best national parks.

**Best for**: Nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, wildlife watchers

**Budget**: $100-180/day

**2026 Highlight**: New Parks Canada initiatives focus on solo traveler safety in wilderness areas.

## How We Chose These Destinations

Our selection considers:

- **Safety ratings**: Official travel advisories and on-the-ground reports
- **Solo-friendliness**: Ease of meeting people, single supplements, and social infrastructure
- **Value for money**: How far your budget stretches
- **Accessibility**: Ease of getting around independently
- **Experience quality**: Unique offerings that shine for solo travelers

Whether you're a first-time solo traveler or a seasoned independent explorer, these destinations offer the perfect conditions for an unforgettable solo adventure in 2026.
