## Vietnam Visa Overview for US Citizens

Since August 2023, US passport holders can enter Vietnam **visa-free for 45 days**. For longer stays, the **e-visa** provides 90 days with multiple entries.

## Current Entry Options

### 1. Visa-Free Entry (45 days)
- **Best for**: Short trips under 45 days
- **Cost**: Free
- **Process**: Just show up with valid passport
- **Entry type**: Single entry

### 2. E-Visa (90 days)
- **Best for**: Extended travel, multiple entries
- **Cost**: $25 USD
- **Process**: Apply online 3-5 days before travel
- **Entry type**: Single or multiple entry available

## Visa-Free Entry Process

Arriving in Vietnam visa-free is simple:

1. **On your flight**, complete the arrival/departure card
2. **At immigration**, present passport and arrival card
3. **Receive stamp** with 45-day validity
4. **Keep departure card** - you'll need it when leaving

## E-Visa Application

For stays over 45 days or needing multiple entries:

### Apply Online
1. Visit [Vietnam E-Visa Portal](https://evisa.gov.vn)
2. Complete application form
3. Upload passport photo and passport data page
4. Pay $25 USD fee
5. Receive e-visa by email in 3 working days

### At the Airport
- Print your e-visa approval letter
- Present at immigration with passport
- Receive entry stamp

## Entry Points

### International Airports
- **Tan Son Nhat (SGN)** - Ho Chi Minh City
- **Noi Bai (HAN)** - Hanoi
- **Da Nang (DAD)** - Central Vietnam
- **Cam Ranh (CXR)** - Nha Trang area
- **Phu Quoc (PQC)** - Island destination

### Popular Land Borders
- **Moc Bai** - From Cambodia (Phnom Penh)
- **Bavet** - From Cambodia
- **Lao Bao** - From Laos
- **Friendship Pass** - From China

## Extending Your Stay

### Extension Process
1. Visit immigration office or use travel agency
2. Submit passport and current visa/stamp
3. Pay fee (~$10-20 USD via agency)
4. Wait 5-7 working days
5. Receive 30-day extension stamp

### Immigration Offices
- **Ho Chi Minh City**: 161 Nguyen Du, District 1
- **Hanoi**: 44-46 Tran Phu, Ba Dinh
- **Da Nang**: 7 Tran Quy Cap

## Solo Traveler Tips

### Money Matters
- ATMs everywhere, but check withdrawal limits
- Many places prefer cash
- Vietnamese dong only - no USD accepted
- Wise/Revolut cards work well

### SIM Cards
- Buy at airport for best convenience
- Viettel, Vinaphone, Mobifone are main providers
- Data plans are extremely cheap (~$5-10 for 30 days)

### Getting Around
- Grab (like Uber) is essential
- Domestic flights are cheap (VietJet, Bamboo)
- Sleeper buses for long distances

## Common Mistakes

1. **Don't forget departure card** - Keep it safe in passport
2. **Don't overstay** - Fines are $25/day
3. **Don't arrive without e-visa printed** - Digital copies may not be accepted at all borders
4. **Don't exchange money at airport** - Rates are terrible
