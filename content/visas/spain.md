## Spain Visa Overview for US Citizens

US passport holders can visit Spain **visa-free for up to 90 days** within any 180-day period as part of the Schengen Agreement.

## The Schengen 90/180 Rule

This is essential to understand:
- **90 days maximum** in any **180-day rolling period**
- Applies to **all 27 Schengen countries combined**
- Spain, Portugal, France, Italy, Germany, etc. all share this limit
- Use a [Schengen calculator](https://ec.europa.eu/home-affairs/content/visa-calculator_en) to track your days

## Entry Process

### Arriving from Outside Schengen
1. Queue at "Non-EU/EEA" passport control
2. Present passport (may be asked about plans, funds, accommodation)
3. Receive Schengen entry stamp
4. Proceed to baggage claim

### Arriving from Another Schengen Country
- No immigration control between Schengen countries
- Your original entry stamp remains valid
- No Spain-specific stamp needed

- Direct flights from most US cities

## ETIAS (Starting 2025)

From 2025, US citizens need **ETIAS authorization** before traveling:
- **Cost**: €7
- **Validity**: 3 years
- **Process**: Online, usually approved quickly
- **Applies to**: All Schengen travel

## Staying Longer: Digital Nomad Visa

Spain's **remote worker visa** is popular for extended stays:

### Requirements
- Monthly income of €2,334+ from non-Spanish sources
- Employment contract or proof of freelance clients
- Health insurance valid in Spain
- Clean criminal record
- Not have lived in Spain last 5 years

### Benefits
- Initial 1-year stay
- Renewable for 2 additional years
- Work legally (for non-Spanish clients)
- Path to residency
- Bring family members

### Application
1. Apply at Spanish consulate in US
2. Submit income proof, insurance, criminal check
3. Wait 20-45 days
4. Travel to Spain with visa
5. Register for NIE (foreigner ID) within 30 days

## Solo Traveler Tips

### Banking
- Wise or Revolut cards work excellently
- Some ATMs charge fees - look for no-fee options
- Cards widely accepted, but carry cash for small purchases

### Transportation
- Renfe trains connect major cities
- Flixbus for budget intercity travel
- Metro systems in Madrid, Barcelona, Valencia
- Domestic flights cheap with Vueling, Ryanair

### Communication
- EU roaming applies if you have European SIM
- Local SIMs: Movistar, Vodafone, Orange
- Strong WiFi in cafes and coworking spaces

## Non-Schengen Side Trips

Reset your Schengen clock by visiting:
- **Morocco** - Just across the strait
- **UK** - Not in Schengen
- **Ireland** - Not in Schengen

Remember: You need to be **outside Schengen for 90 days** before a new 90-day period begins.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Forgetting Schengen days accumulate** - Track all European travel
2. **Assuming 90 days per country** - It's 90 days total for all Schengen
3. **Not having onward travel proof** - Immigration may ask
4. **Overstaying** - Affects future Schengen travel
