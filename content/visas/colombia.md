## Colombia Visa Overview for US Citizens

US passport holders can visit Colombia **visa-free for up to 90 days**, with the option to extend for another 90 days. The maximum stay per calendar year is 180 days.

## Before You Travel

### Check-Mig Registration (Mandatory)
Complete this **24-72 hours** before your flight:
1. Visit [Migración Colombia](https://apps.migracioncolombia.gov.co/pre-registro/)
2. Fill in personal details and flight information
3. Enter your Colombian address
4. Save the QR code generated
5. Show QR code at immigration

**Don't skip this!** Airlines may check for Check-Mig confirmation.

### Proof of Onward Travel
Colombia **strictly enforces** this requirement:
- Airlines often check before boarding
- Immigration will ask for it
- Options: flight, bus to Ecuador/Peru, or boat ticket
- In a pinch: refundable ticket or services like OnwardTicket

## Entry Process

1. **Complete Check-Mig** before travel
2. **At immigration**, show passport and Check-Mig QR code
3. **Answer questions** about your visit (purpose, where staying)
4. **Receive stamp** with 90-day authorization
5. **Keep entry stamp accessible** - may need for hotel check-ins

## Major Entry Points

### Bogotá
- **El Dorado Airport (BOG)** - Main international hub
- Connects to all domestic destinations
- Located at high altitude (2,640m) - acclimate first

### Medellín
- **José María Córdova Airport (MDE)** - International flights
- Located in Rionegro, 45 min from city
- Growing number of US direct flights

### Cartagena
- **Rafael Núñez Airport (CTG)** - Caribbean gateway
- Direct flights from Miami, Fort Lauderdale
- Colonial city experience

### Other Airports
- **Cali (CLO)** - Salsa capital
- **Barranquilla (BAQ)** - Caribbean coast
- **Santa Marta (SMR)** - Near Tayrona

## Extending Your Stay

### 90-Day Extension Process
1. Visit Migración Colombia office **before** current stay expires
2. Bring: passport, passport photos, proof of funds, accommodation
3. Pay extension fee (~$50-60 USD)
4. Receive new stamp for additional 90 days

### Migración Colombia Offices
- **Bogotá**: Multiple locations
- **Medellín**: Centro Administrativo La Alpujarra
- **Cartagena**: Centro, Calle del Arsenal

### Important Limitations
- **Maximum 180 days per calendar year**
- After 180 days, must leave Colombia until next year
- No "border runs" to reset the clock within same year

## Digital Nomad Visa (V-Type)

Colombia offers a specific remote worker visa:

### Requirements
- Monthly income of $3+ million COP (~$750 USD)
- Letter from employer or clients
- Health insurance
- Clean criminal record
- Passport valid 180+ days

### Benefits
- 2-year initial validity
- Renewable
- Can open bank account
- Path to longer-term residency
- Work legally for foreign clients

### Application
- Apply at Colombian consulate before travel, or
- Apply within Colombia if entering on tourist status
- Processing time: 5-30 days

## Solo Traveler Tips

### Safety
Colombia has improved dramatically, but:
- Use Uber, Beat, or DiDi instead of street taxis
- Avoid flashy jewelry/electronics
- Stay in recommended neighborhoods
- Keep phone in front pocket or bag
- Don't accept drinks from strangers

### Recommended Neighborhoods
**Medellín**: Poblado, Laureles, Envigado
**Bogotá**: Chapinero, Zona G, Usaquén, Candelaria (daytime)
**Cartagena**: Walled City, Getsemaní, Bocagrande

### Money
- Colombian peso (COP)
- ATMs everywhere (use those inside banks)
- Wise/Revolut cards work well
- Many places accept USD but rate is poor
- Carry some cash for small purchases

### Connectivity
- Claro, Movistar, Tigo SIM cards available
- Data plans very affordable
- Good WiFi in cafes and coworking spaces
- WeWork and Selina popular for coworking

## Popular Solo Travel Routes

### The Classic Circuit
1. **Bogotá** (3-4 days) - Museums, food, coffee
2. **Salento/Coffee Region** (3-4 days) - Coffee farms, Valle de Cocora
3. **Medellín** (1-2 weeks) - City life, day trips
4. **Cartagena** (4-5 days) - Colonial history, beaches

### Caribbean Coast
- Cartagena → Santa Marta → Tayrona → Lost City Trek
- Add Palomino for beach time

### Adventure Route
- San Gil (adventure sports)
- Los Nevados (hiking)
- Tatacoa Desert
- San Agustín (archaeology)

## Common Mistakes

1. **Skipping Check-Mig** - Can cause boarding denial
2. **No onward ticket** - Entry denial is possible
3. **Overstaying** - Fines and future entry problems
4. **Carrying all cash** - Use cards, limit cash on hand
5. **Ignoring altitude** - Bogotá is 2,640m, acclimate first
