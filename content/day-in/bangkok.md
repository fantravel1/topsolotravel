faqs:
  - question: "Is Bangkok safe for solo travelers?"
    answer: "Bangkok is very safe for solo travelers. Violent crime against tourists is rare. The main concerns are scams (gem scams, inflated tuk-tuk prices), pickpockets in crowds, and traffic. Use common sense, don't accept unsolicited 'help' from strangers, and you'll likely have zero issues."
  - question: "How much does a day in Bangkok cost?"
    answer: "Bangkok is incredibly affordable. A full day costs ‡∏ø1,000-1,500 ($30-45) including temple entries, meals, transport, and activities. You can go lower by eating only street food and walking more, or higher by adding luxury like rooftop bars and spa treatments."
  - question: "What should I wear in Bangkok temples?"
    answer: "Cover shoulders and knees at all temples. Long pants or skirts below the knee, and shirts with sleeves (not tank tops). Many temples provide wraps if you forget, but it's easier to dress appropriately. Remove shoes before entering temple buildings."
  - question: "How do I handle Bangkok street food safely?"
    answer: "Look for stalls with high turnover (ingredients stay fresh), visible cooking (not pre-made sitting out), and local crowds. Avoid salads and cut fruit from questionable sources. When in doubt, watch where locals eat. After 15 years of street food, Bangkok's vendors know food safety."

## Your Day in Bangkok Awaits

Bangkok doesn't ease you in ‚Äî it swallows you whole.

The heat, the noise, the smells, the impossible traffic, the glittering temples rising from chaos ‚Äî it's all happening at once, all the time. And somehow, in this beautiful madness, solo travel feels easier than anywhere else.

For $30-40, you'll eat like royalty on plastic stools, visit temples that make you believe in something, and discover a city that never apologizes for being exactly what it is. Bangkok doesn't care if you're alone. It's too busy being alive.
