faqs:

    answer: "Bali is exceptional for solo travelers. The island is incredibly safe, locals are warm and welcoming, and there's a massive solo travel community (especially in Ubud and Canggu) making it easy to find connection. Many activities ‚Äî yoga, spa, temple visits ‚Äî are naturally solo-friendly. You'll never feel alone unless you want to."
  - question: "How much does a day in Bali cost?"
    answer: "Bali is remarkably affordable. A comfortable day costs $30-60 including accommodation, meals at warungs, transport, one activity (spa/yoga/temple), and drinks. You can go cheaper ($15-20 budget style) or much higher at beach clubs and fancy restaurants. The value compared to Western destinations is incredible."
  - question: "Is Ubud or Seminyak/Canggu better for solo travelers?"
    answer: "Ubud is better for wellness, spirituality, rice terraces, and cultural immersion. The vibe is calm and introspective. Canggu/Seminyak offer beaches, surfing, nightlife, and a more social backpacker scene. Many solo travelers split time between both. Ubud for soul, Canggu for social."
  - question: "Do I need a scooter in Bali?"
    answer: "It depends on your comfort level. Scooters transform Bali ‚Äî freedom to explore, see hidden spots, and travel cheaply ($5/day). But traffic is chaotic and accidents are common. Alternatives: Grab/Gojek ride-hailing, private drivers ($40-50/day), or staying in walkable areas. Many solo travelers visit without ever riding."

## Your Day in Bali Awaits

Bali is not one place ‚Äî it's a hundred places disguised as an island.

There's the Bali of yoga retreats and crystal shops. The Bali of ancient temples where incense rises daily. The Bali of surfers and beach clubs. The Bali of rice terraces that make your heart ache. The Bali of warungs where $2 buys the best meal of your life.

As a solo traveler, you get to find which Bali is yours. Maybe all of them. The island welcomes seekers ‚Äî whether you're seeking enlightenment, adventure, community, or just a really good massage.

The only wrong way to do Bali is to rush it.
